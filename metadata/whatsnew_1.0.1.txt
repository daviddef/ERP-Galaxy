A much bigger answer to "what happens to my tables in S/4HANA" — down to the column, with somewhere to put what you find.

NEW — WALKTHROUGH AND APP GUIDE
First launch now opens a short walkthrough of what the app does, and an App guide in the menu covers every feature — including, deliberately, what the app will not tell you.

CORRECTED MIGRATION VERDICTS
Worth stating plainly. Tables in the wider index that carry a published SAP verdict were being shown as "carries over". They are not. Disappears went from 11 to 70 once they were read properly. Separately, VBUK and VBUP read "unchanged" where SAP had eliminated them, and MARC and MARD read "unchanged" where SAP says compatibility view only. Where our wording disagreed with SAP's Simplification List, SAP now wins.

Tables with no SAP entry at all are now their own bucket — "No verdict" — rather than counted as safe. Absence of an item is not a promise.

NEW — PAYROLL CLUSTER TABLES, PROPERLY
PCL3 and PCL4 were missing from the app entirely; PCL1 had no description. All four are now there with their RELID areas, plus HRPY_RGDIR and the declustered payroll tables.

More to the point, the app used to offer key fields and joins on these as though you could SELECT them. You cannot — the payload is one compressed binary field. Cluster tables now say so first, before anything else, and the ABAP generator gives you IMPORT FROM DATABASE instead of a join that could never work. PCL2 also shows what is actually inside the payload — RT, CRT, WPBP, BT, VERSC — and what PC_PAYRESULT is really doing.

CORRECTED — PCL2 IN S/4HANA
It said "Unchanged in S/4." True, and beside the point. SAP's end of support for pool and cluster tables in 1809 does not apply to these, because they are transparent tables holding an application-level cluster rather than Dictionary table clusters. What changes is entitlement, not the table. Declustering is optional, additive, and older than S/4HANA.

NEW — FIELD GUIDE
A written ECC → S/4HANA guide, generated from the app's own data so its numbers always match what the app holds. Readable offline, and shareable.

NEW — LESSONS LEARNED
23 lessons. 16 come from SAP's own required-action text, which repeats across dozens of tables — one instruction covers 35 finance tables. 7 are our own conclusions, marked as such. 141 tables show the lessons that apply to them.

NEW — TO-DO LIST
Add tables, playbooks, field changes, decoded codes and your own notes to one working list, grouped into a suggested order. Tick off, reorder, share as text, or export a PDF that carries the full detail for every table.

NEW — BAPI AND FUNCTION MODULE CATALOGUE
3,622 BAPIs and RFC-enabled modules with their application component and cloud release status. Searchable in business language — try "work order", "goods movement", "purchase order".

NEW — FIELD-LEVEL CHANGES
Six systemic changes, each with SAP's Note number: material number CHAR 18 → 40, currency amounts to 23 digits, VBTYP → VBTYPL, object list number INT4 → INT8, segment 16 → 40, season → 10.

NEW — JOIN FINDER
Every table shows what it joins to and the exact fields to join ON, with confidence marked.

ALSO NEW
Paste a table list to chart only those. Save the whole working state as a named view. Park the detail panel mid-screen and keep browsing.

FIXED — RELATIONSHIPS NOW DRAWN ON THE CHART
Tables listed relationships in their panel that the chart never drew — 26 of them, including ACDOCA to T001 and LIKP to VBAK. The chart and the panel were reading two different lists. They now read the same one.

FIXED
Long table names no longer spill outside their circle on the chart. The ECC6 / S/4HANA filter applies to search as well as the chart, and the buttons show the filter you are actually on. Movement type 281 was cut off mid-sentence.

2,000+ tables, still completely offline, still no account, still no data collection.
