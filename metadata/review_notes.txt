3) PURPOSE AND TARGET AUDIENCE
Audience: SAP consultants, data architects, ABAP developers, migration teams.

Problem: SAP systems hold thousands of tables with cryptic names (BSEG, ACDOCA). Practitioners need to know what a table holds, its key fields, and how it relates to others. Separately, SAP ends mainstream maintenance for ECC on 31 December 2027, so teams must work out which tables survive migration to S/4HANA.

Value: the app answers both offline. 217 core tables as an interactive relationship graph, plus a searchable index of 1,816 more with plain-English descriptions, primary keys and foreign keys. Users select the tables they care about and get a migration verdict for each - disappears, replaced, becomes a compatibility view, changes, or carries over - with the supporting quote from SAP's published Simplification List.

4) HOW TO ACCESS THE MAIN FEATURES
No setup, credentials or sample files needed. From a cold launch:
- The graph appears immediately. Tap any circle for that table's detail sheet.
- Type BSEG in the search field, tap the result.
- Tap the padlock on a table to add it to your board. Add several.
- Open the menu (top right), tap "Impact" for the migration verdict on your selection.
- The menu also holds module filters, a Levels slider, a Classic/Fun reading toggle and a legend.
- Optional: add the "Table of the Day" widget, or search BSEG in iOS Spotlight.

5) EXTERNAL SERVICES, TOOLS OR PLATFORMS
None. Zero network requests at runtime; it runs identically in Airplane Mode. No data providers, authentication, payment processors, AI services, analytics or advertising SDKs.

All reference data ships inside the app bundle as static files. The relationship graph is drawn by a bundled local copy of D3.js (open source, BSD-3-Clause), loaded from the bundle, not a network, purely as a local drawing library.

Reference content was compiled by us before release from publicly available material, including SAP's published Simplification List. All descriptive text in the app was written by us in our own words.

6) REGIONAL DIFFERENCES
None. Identical in all regions. English only, no region-locked content, no regional pricing, no geolocation. The same bundled dataset everywhere.
